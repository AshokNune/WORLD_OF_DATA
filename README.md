# The AI Hierarchy: Technical Definitions

This document details the hierarchical relationship between Artificial Intelligence concepts, moving from the broadest category to the most specific technology.

`AI > ML > NN > DL > GenAI > LLMs`

---

## 1. Artificial Intelligence (AI)
**The Broad Concept**
AI is the general field of computer science dedicated to creating systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence.
* **Scope:** It covers everything "smart" a computer does, regardless of how it achieves it (rules vs. learning).
* **Key Characteristic:** It acts intelligently.
* **Example:** A chess engine that uses brute-force search algorithms to calculate the best move.

## 2. Machine Learning (ML)
**The Method**
ML is a specific subset of AI where the system **learns** from data rather than following explicit, hard-coded rules.
* **Scope:** Algorithms that optimize their performance based on historical data.
* **Key Characteristic:** It improves with experience (training data).
* **Example:** A spam filter that learns to identify junk email by analyzing thousands of examples flagged by users.

## 3. Neural Networks (NN)
**The Architecture**
Neural Networks are a specific class of Machine Learning models inspired by the biological structure of the human brain.
* **Scope:** Models built using layers of interconnected nodes (neurons).
* **Key Characteristic:** Data is processed through weighted connections, allowing the model to capture non-linear relationships.
* **Example:** A credit scoring model that weighs multiple inputs (income, debt, age) simultaneously to predict default risk.

## 4. Deep Learning (DL)
**The Power**
Deep Learning refers to Neural Networks that are "Deep"â€”meaning they have many hidden layers (dozens or hundreds) between the input and output.
* **Scope:** High-capacity networks capable of learning from massive, unstructured datasets.
* **Key Characteristic:** Automated "Feature Extraction." The model figures out what is important (e.g., edges, textures) without human guidance.
* **Example:** FaceID on a smartphone, which analyzes complex pixel patterns to recognize a face.

## 5. Generative AI (GenAI)
**The Function**
GenAI is a subset of Deep Learning models designed to **create new content** rather than just classify existing data.
* **Scope:** Models that output new artifacts (Images, Audio, Code) that resemble the training data.
* **Key Characteristic:** It moves from "Discriminative" (Is this a cat?) to "Generative" (Draw a cat).
* **Example:** Midjourney or DALL-E generating an image from a text prompt.

## 6. Large Language Models (LLMs)
**The Specialist**
LLMs are Generative AI models trained specifically on massive amounts of **text data**.
* **Scope:** Models designed to understand, predict, and generate human language.
* **Key Characteristic:** They function as "Next-Token Predictors," calculating the statistical probability of the next word in a sequence.
* **Example:** GPT-4, Claude, or Gemini.

## Summary: The "Drill Down" Effect

Here is how a single business problem moves through these layers:

| Layer | The Decision / Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1. AI** | "We want to **automate customer service**." |
| **2. ML** | "We will use a system that **learns from past logs** instead of writing manual scripts." |
| **3. NN** | "We will use a **Neural Network** because the relationships in language are complex." |
| **4. DL** | "We need **Deep Learning** because we want to process complex, unstructured conversation history." |
| **5. GenAI** | "We want the bot to **generate unique responses** rather than picking from a pre-set list." |
| **6. LLM** | "We will deploy **GPT-4** specifically because it is the best at handling **text**." |
